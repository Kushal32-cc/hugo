What is Ready Mix Concrete

Ready-mix concrete is produced in a centralized batching plant where raw materials—cement, aggregates, water, and admixtures—are weighed and combined by computer-controlled systems. Because the entire process occurs in a controlled environment, the mix proportions are exact and repeatable, eliminating the variability that often plagues on-site hand-mixing. Continuous laboratory testing at the plant ensures that the concrete leaving the gate meets the specified strength, workability, and durability requirements.

Delivery in Transit Mixers

Once batched, the concrete is loaded into revolving drum trucks—transit mixers—that keep the mixture agitated while it travels to the jobsite. This closed-drum transport prevents segregation of aggregates and moisture loss, so the material arrives uniform and ready for immediate placement. The logistics team can track each truck's route and arrival time, allowing contractors to schedule pours precisely and minimize site downtime.

Customizable Mix Designs

Because batching is recipe-based, producers can tweak cement content, water-cement ratio, aggregate grading, or chemical admixtures to create specialty concretes: high-early-strength mixes for rapid form stripping, sulfate-resistant mixes for marine environments, self-compacting concrete for congested reinforcement, or fiber-reinforced options for crack control. This flexibility lets engineers specify exactly what the project needs without extra on-site additives.
